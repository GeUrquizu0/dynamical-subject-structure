Step 2: From Complexity Metrics to the Dynamic Structure of the Subject

Author: J.G. Urquizu O.
Date: March 2026
Field: Computational Neuroscience / Complex Systems

Summary

This document develops a conceptual and methodological framework to identify and characterize dynamic attractors in neural state spaces based on the Σ* metric. It covers temporal window selection, state-space reconstruction, geometric and dynamic analysis, stability, recurrence, and robustness. Falsifiable predictions regarding consciousness dynamics are provided, preparing for the conceptual transfer of the subject in Step 3.

Contents

1. Objectives – Confirm organized activity, geometry, dimensionality, robustness, and falsifiable predictions.


2. Conceptual Pipeline – Methodological sequence: window selection, space reconstruction, geometric analysis, stability, recurrence, robustness, and relation to cognitive states.


3. Falsifiable Predictions – Expected attractor behavior and recovery from perturbations.


4. Conceptual Formalization – Theoretical representation of attractor confinement, convergence, and robustness.


5. Outputs and Applications – Attractor maps, stability indicators, and predictions of state transitions.


6. Conceptual Figures – Conceptual diagrams in place of experimental plots.



> Step 2 focuses on conceptual and methodological preparation. Diagrams are illustrative; no experimental data are included.
